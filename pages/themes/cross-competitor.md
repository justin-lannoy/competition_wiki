# Cross-Competitor Themes & Signals

_Synthesized from the 24-month SEC filings + recent news pull (June 2026). Figures
are point-in-time; see each competitor's SEC tracker for sourcing. Maintained as
analyst intelligence — update after each refresh._

## Common themes across the peer set

### 1. Tariff / trade-policy risk — newly universal
A new or elevated forward-looking risk in nearly every FY2025/early-2026 filing —
merchandise cost and non-prime discretionary income. Cited by [[acima]] (Upbound),
[[progressive-leasing]], [[american-first-finance]] (FirstCash), [[affirm]],
[[synchrony-financial]], [[bread-financial]], and [[afterpay]] (Block).

### 2. Enforcement shifting from a retreating CFPB to state AGs
Federal CFPB paused/gutted and its late-fee rule vacated — but state pressure rose:
the multistate AG action against [[acima]], a 7-state AG + Senate inquiry into
[[sezzle]] and other BNPL firms, a six-provider BNPL inquiry touching
[[progressive-leasing]], NY's BNPL ability-to-repay licensing, and Block's DOJ
accrual. The venue is moving from federal to state AGs and private litigants.

### 3. AI underwriting as a formal, standalone risk factor
[[acima]] (Upbound), [[progressive-leasing]], [[affirm]] (models trained on ~343M
loans), [[afterpay]] (Block), and [[klarna]] all newly added explicit
AI/generative-AI risk language (model degradation, bias, evolving regulation).

### 4. Verticalization beyond core LTO/BNPL
[[progressive-leasing]] into payroll-deduction benefits (Purchasing Power);
[[acima]] (Upbound) into earned-wage access (Brigit); [[american-first-finance]]
and [[kafene]] into automotive/tire; [[synchrony-financial]] into health/pet
(CareCredit), power-sports, Walmart/OnePay; [[sezzle]] into stored-value and even
mobile (MVNO); [[healthcare-finance-direct]] in patient financing.

### 5. BNPL → balance-sheet lending convergence
[[afterpay]] (Block) reclassified Cash App Borrow/Afterpay loans to
held-for-investment via its ILC; [[affirm]] scaling interest-bearing installment
loans + securitizations; [[sezzle]] weighing an ILC charter. BNPL players are
migrating toward owning regulated, on-balance-sheet credit — encroaching on Snap's
installment/LTO economics.

### 6. Funding-cost repricing is widespread
Elevated coupons across the set: [[progressive-leasing]]'s Purchasing Power ABS
(4.37–7.54%); [[american-first-finance]] (FirstCash) $750M 6.125% notes; [[sezzle]]'s
$300M facility at SOFR+3.86%; [[bread-financial]] 8.625%/8.875% preferreds;
[[synchrony-financial]] 7.25% preferred; [[katapult]]'s distressed 11.3% revolver +
18% preferred. Scaled players (Affirm securitizations, Synchrony/Bread ~48% deposit
funding) hold a structural cost advantage over LTO specialists like Snap.

## Financial signals by competitor

### Direct LTO competitors (head-to-head for Snap's customer)
- **[[acima]] (Upbound / UPBD):** growth decelerating (Acima rev +1.8% YoY vs +10.5%
  two quarters earlier); charge-offs eating the gains. **43-state AG investigation +
  NY AG suit** is the single biggest competitive/regulatory event — legal accruals
  ballooned to **$74.2M** (from $20.7M), "agreement in principle" near. A multistate
  consent order could reshape LTO disclosure/fee practices industry-wide.
- **[[progressive-leasing]] (PRG):** GMV **−10% YoY**, active customers shrinking;
  deliberately diversifying out of LTO via Purchasing Power ($424M, guiding LTO
  below 70% of revenue).
- **[[american-first-finance]] (FCFS):** finance-receivable **delinquency up to
  22.4%**; pivoting to bank-partner installment loans (now 45% of GTV) and auto/tire
  (auto 25%, furniture collapsed 48%→23%).
- **[[katapult]] (KPLT):** **going-concern, 11 covenant waivers, merging into
  Aaron's/CCFI** — a weakened, distracted e-commerce LTO competitor = opportunity
  for Snap.

### Adjacent BNPL (compete upstream in the waterfall)
- **[[affirm]] (AFRM):** profitable, GMV $36.7B, **$2.85B securitizations in 9
  months** — a funding-cost advantage Snap can't match.
- **[[sezzle]] (SEZL):** GMV **+37%**, provisions rising, exploring an ILC charter;
  new facility has a hard 3.75% loss-rate tripwire.
- **[[afterpay]] (Block / XYZ):** moving consumer loans onto its balance sheet via
  its ILC ($3.3B HFI), embedding BNPL everywhere; losses surging.

### Prime card issuers (structurally better-funded)
- **[[synchrony-financial]] (SYF):** credit **improving** (NCO 5.42%, down 96 bps);
  building a POS stack (Ally Lending, Versatile Credit, Lowe's) adjacent to Snap.
- **[[bread-financial]] (BFH):** repricing up (loan yield 27%), de-risking; DTC
  deposits now 48% of funding.

## Snap-specific read-throughs
- **Merchant-bankruptcy contagion** (Big Lots, American Signature/Value City,
  American Freight, Conn's) suppressed LTO volume at [[progressive-leasing]] and
  [[american-first-finance]] — expect the same furniture-vertical air pocket.
- **FlexShopper was acquired by Snap's ReadySett subsidiary** (Mar 2026), and the
  FlexShopper→[[katapult]] patent suit settled June 2026 — Snap is an active player
  in the competitive/IP chess, not just an observer. (FlexShopper is therefore *not*
  tracked here as a competitor.)
