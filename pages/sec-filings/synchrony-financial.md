**Synchrony Financial (SYF) — 10-K for Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2025**
*Filed February 6, 2026 | Relevant to Snap Finance as a point-of-sale financing competitor*

---

#### Key financials
- Total loan receivables of **$103.8 billion** at December 31, 2025; purchase volume of **$182.3 billion** for full-year 2025; active accounts of **70.7 million**.
- Deposits of **$81.1 billion** (84% of total funding sources) at December 31, 2025, comprised of **$75.2 billion direct** and **$5.9 billion brokered**; CD renewal retention rate of **86%** for 2025.
- Five largest partner programs (Amazon, Lowe's, PayPal, Sam's Club, TJX) collectively represented **54% of total interest and fees on loans** and **52% of loan receivables** at December 31, 2025.
- Digital platform (30% of interest/fees) and Home & Auto (26%) were the two largest sales platforms; Lifestyle (installment-loan-heavy, relevant to Snap's space) was smallest at **5% (~$1.1 billion)**.

---

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Synchrony operates as a **single reportable segment**; results managed across five sales platforms (Home & Auto, Digital, Diversified & Value, Health & Wellness, Lifestyle) — no separate segment P&L disclosed.
- Installment loans (closest analog to Snap's lease-to-own/POS model) represented **5.3% of total loan receivables** at December 31, 2025, primarily secured power-sports loans in the Lifestyle platform and unsecured "Pay Later" products across all platforms; deferred-interest credit cards remain dominant at **17.7%** of receivables.
- In **September 2025**, Synchrony launched the **OnePay/Walmart** exclusive general-purpose and private-label credit card program, materially expanding its mass-market/value retail footprint — a competitive pressure point for installment lenders serving Walmart-adjacent merchants.
- Competition disclosure cites banks, credit unions, fintech lenders, and BNPL providers as primary rivals; company emphasizes **~60% digital application rate** and omnichannel integration as key differentiators vs. point-of-sale specialists like Snap.

---

#### Risk-factor changes
- **CFPB late-fee rule** (previously a major risk) was **vacated in April 2025**; the 10-K notes the rule's removal but flags ongoing regulatory uncertainty, including new constraints tied to Synchrony crossing **$100 billion in total assets**, triggering enhanced prudential standards.
- New/elevated forward-looking risk language around **tariffs and retaliatory tariffs**, **federal government shutdowns** (cited October–November 2025 shutdowns as a specific example), and **U.S. presidential administration policy changes** on fiscal, monetary, and consumer-finance regulation.
- Continued emphasis on **credit loss / CECL model accuracy** as a key risk, with loan modification activity (long-term and short-term) tracked across credit card, consumer loan, and commercial portfolios — signaling elevated borrower stress management through 2025.

---

#### Material events
- **March 1, 2024**: Closed acquisition of **Ally Lending** (Ally Financial's point-of-sale financing business), directly adding a competing POS installment loan platform to Synchrony's capabilities — integration ongoing through 2025.
- **March 2024**: Sold **Pets Best Insurance** to Independence Pet Holdings, Inc. (IPH); retained an **equity stake in IPH** carried on balance sheet at December 31, 2025; CareCredit integrated with Pets Best and Pumpkin Insurance for direct claims reimbursement.
- **2025**: Extended program agreement with **Amazon** and announced acquisition of **Lowe's commercial co-branded credit card portfolio**, expected to close in **first half of 2026**.
- **July 2025**: Renewed/amended **unsecured revolving credit facility**; also established **new share repurchase authorization in April 2025** with an incremental authorization added **September 2025**, reflecting capital return activity during the year.
