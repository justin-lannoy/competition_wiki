## Filing summaries

### 8-K — 2026-06-08

#### Key financials
- Settlement requires Katapult to pay a lump-sum amount to Flexshopper; the exact dollar figure was not disclosed in the filing.
- In exchange, Katapult receives a nonexclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, fully paid-up, royalty-free, worldwide license to the five Subject Patents—no ongoing royalty obligations.

#### Segment & competitive notes
- The settled lawsuit was brought by Flexshopper, Inc. (a direct lease-to-own competitor), which filed suit on September 30, 2024 in the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Texas (Case No. 2:24-cv-00795-JRG) alleging infringement of five patents (U.S. Patent Nos. 10,089,682; 10,282,778; 10,891,687; 11,966,969; 12,067,611).
- The patent license explicitly covers Katapult's affiliates, including Aaron's Intermediate HoldCo, Inc. and CCF Holdings LLC (CCFI) and their affiliates, suggesting Katapult's corporate family has broader LTO platform exposure protected by this settlement.

#### Risk-factor changes
- Litigation risk from this specific patent dispute is eliminated; Flexshopper and ReadySett LLC granted a full covenant not to sue Katapult and its affiliates/successors for direct or indirect infringement of the Subject Patents.
- No new risk factors were introduced in this filing; the company references its most recent 10-Q filed March 31, 2026 for standing risk disclosures.

#### Material events
- On June 5, 2026, Katapult reached a settlement agreement resolving Flexshopper, Inc. v. Katapult Holdings, Inc.; claims were dismissed with prejudice on June 8, 2026.
- The settlement entity included ReadySett LLC, the third-party purchaser of the Flexshopper patents, ensuring the license and covenant not to sue extend to the current patent owner.

### 8-K — 2026-06-03

## Katapult Holdings, Inc. (KPLT) — 8-K Filed June 3, 2026

#### Key financials
- No financial results disclosed in this filing.
- The Third Amendment reduces the advance rate under the Amended and Restated Loan and Security Agreement (originally dated June 12, 2025); specific advance rate levels are not disclosed in the filing text.

#### Segment & competitive notes
- None noted.

#### Risk-factor changes
- Katapult has executed **eleven separate limited waivers** under its credit facility since September 15, 2025, indicating sustained and repeated covenant compliance issues over an approximately eight-month period.
- The Third Amendment (June 2, 2026) removes the **Minimum Trailing Net Three-Month Originations** covenant, suggesting originations have been running below previously required thresholds — a negative signal for lease-to-own volume trends relevant to Snap Finance's competitive monitoring.

#### Material events
- On **June 2, 2026**, Katapult SPV-1 LLC, Katapult Group, Inc., and Katapult Holdings, Inc. entered into the **Third Amendment and Limited Waiver** to their Amended and Restated Loan and Security Agreement with lender/agent **Midtown Madison Management LLC**.
- Key changes: (1) removal of the Minimum Trailing Net Three-Month Originations requirement and (2) reduction of the advance rate — both terms suggesting the lender accommodated deteriorating operating performance in exchange for tighter collateral coverage.

### 10-Q — 2026-05-08

#### Key financials
- Q1 2026 total revenue was $79.0M (+9.8% vs. $71.9M in Q1 2025); gross profit was $18.2M (23.0% margin) vs. $14.3M (19.9%) in Q1 2025; net income was $5.7M vs. net loss of $(5.7)M in Q1 2025.
- Adjusted EBITDA was $6.4M in Q1 2026 vs. $2.2M in Q1 2025; operating cash flow improved to $12.2M from $3.4M year-over-year.
- Revolving credit balance declined to $71.6M at March 31, 2026 (from $78.7M at December 31, 2025); facility bears interest at 11.3% (SOFR + 7.0%, 3.0% floor) and matures December 4, 2026 — within 12 months, triggering a going-concern disclosure.
- Net income available to common stockholders was only $0.4M ($0.07/share) after allocating $5.0M to Series A/B Convertible Preferred Stock dividends and undistributed earnings under the two-class method.

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Katapult operates as a single reportable segment (U.S. lease-to-own); gross originations were essentially flat at $64.2M in Q1 2026 vs. $64.2M in Q1 2025 (+0.1%).
- KPay's share of gross originations grew to 42% in Q1 2026 from 35% in Q1 2025, partially offsetting Wayfair's declining contribution (18% of gross originations in Q1 2026 vs. 27% in Q1 2025).
- On March 3, 2026, FlexShopper (which filed a patent-infringement suit against Katapult in September 2024) was sold out of Chapter 11 bankruptcy to ReadySett, LLC, a subsidiary of **Snap Finance** — directly transferring the litigation to a key Snap Finance entity; Katapult is vigorously defending.

#### Risk-factor changes
- Going-concern risk is explicitly flagged: the $110M New Revolving Facility matures December 4, 2026, and Katapult lacks sufficient cash to repay it without refinancing; multiple covenant waivers were required (most recently April 15 and May 5, 2026) for failure to meet the Minimum Trailing Three-Month Net Originations covenant.
- Merger-related risk is a newly prominent category: the pending mergers with CCFI (Aaron's parent) and Aaron's — announced December 11, 2025 and expected to close in Q3 2026 — would leave existing Katapult stockholders with only ~6.0% of the combined company, with CCFI equityholders holding ~79.9%.
- Preferred stock risk is new vs. prior periods: Series A/B Convertible Preferred Stock accrues dividends at 18% annually (potentially rising to 19% compounding weekly if stockholder approval for the Hawthorn exchange is not obtained), with combined liquidation preference of ~$70.0M as of March 31, 2026.

#### Material events
- On December 11, 2025, Katapult signed a Merger Agreement with CCFI and Aaron's; the deal is expected to close in Q3 2026 and resulted in $1.7M of transaction costs in Q1 2026; a $1.5M termination fee would be owed to CCFI/Aaron's under certain break scenarios.
- On November 3, 2025, Katapult issued 35,000 shares of Series A and 30,000 shares of Series B Convertible Preferred Stock to Hawthorn for $65.0M aggregate proceeds, used to extinguish the term loan; a compound embedded derivative (Convertible Preferred Derivative) was bifurcated and carried at $9.3M fair value as of March 31, 2026, down from $13.6M at December 31, 2025, generating a $4.3M non-cash gain in Q1 2026.
- Subsequent to quarter-end, the Company entered into covenant waiver agreements on April 15, 2026 and May 5, 2026, each waiving failures to meet the Minimum Trailing Three-Month Net Originations covenant, underscoring ongoing liquidity pressure ahead of the December 2026 facility maturity.

### 10-K — 2026-03-11

#### Key financials
- Total aggregate indebtedness under the Loan Agreement was approximately **$78.7 million** principal outstanding as of December 31, 2025; minimum liquidity covenant requires at least **$5.0 million** in unrestricted cash at all times.
- Wayfair concentration declined to **25%** of gross originations in FY2025 (down from **36%** in FY2024); top 10 merchants collectively represented **76%** of gross originations in FY2025 (vs. **78%** in FY2024).
- Market cap of non-affiliate float was approximately **$23.3 million** based on June 30, 2025 closing price of **$8.01**; shares outstanding as of March 9, 2026 were **4,765,058**.
- KPay transactions grew to **42%** of total gross originations for FY2025; approximately **62%** of 2025 gross originations originated in the Katapult App.

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Single operating segment (lease-to-own); serves nonprime U.S. consumers in 46 states plus D.C. in an estimated **$50–$60 billion** TAM, with self-described market share below **1%** based on 2025 gross originations.
- Competes against national/regional LTO store operators, virtual LTO companies, BNPL providers, installment lenders, and consumer finance companies; Katapult differentiates via no late fees, no NSF fees, and e-commerce-first technology; net promoter score was **46** and repeat purchase rate was **64%** as of December 31, 2025.

#### Risk-factor changes
- New merger-specific risk category added covering the December 11, 2025 definitive agreement with CCFI and Aaron's: risks include deal failure, ownership dilution (existing Katapult shareholders would own only ~**6%** of combined entity on a fully diluted basis post-close), and a **$1.5 million** termination fee payable to CCFI/Aaron's under certain circumstances.
- New preferred stock risk category added following the November 3, 2025 issuance of **35,000 shares** of Series A and **30,000 shares** of Series B Convertible Preferred Stock to Hawthorn; dividends accrue at **18% per annum compounding weekly** until Preferred Stock Investment Stockholder Approval is obtained (rate escalates to **19%** if approval not received by the Dividend Step-up Deadline; floor of **12%** thereafter).
