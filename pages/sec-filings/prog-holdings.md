#### Key financials
- Progressive Leasing comprised ~96% of PROG Holdings' consolidated revenues for FY2025; the company does not disclose total revenue in the truncated filing text, but the two-segment structure (Progressive Leasing + Four) reflects continuing operations after Vive was classified as discontinued.
- Personnel costs (excluding stock-based compensation) were $153.9M in 2025, down from $157.4M in 2024 and $170.8M in 2023, reflecting ongoing workforce reductions.
- Provision for lease merchandise write-offs as a percentage of lease revenues held steady at 7.5% in both 2025 and 2024, up from 6.7% in 2023; targeted range remains 6%–8%.
- Top-3 POS partners generated 54.8% of consolidated revenues in 2025; top-10 generated 77.0%.

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Progressive Leasing operates through ~24,000 third-party POS partner locations across 45 states, D.C., and Puerto Rico; Furniture/Appliances/Electronics stable at 58% of segment revenue for three consecutive years (2023–2025).
- Four (BNPL segment) shifted revenue mix: Transaction Income fell to 46% of Four revenues in 2025 from 56% in 2024 and 84% in 2023, while Subscription Revenue grew to 29% from 23% in 2024 (none in 2023), signaling a deliberate model transition.
- Vive Financial (second-look revolving credit) sold substantially all loan receivables on October 20, 2025 and reclassified as discontinued operations; Vive is no longer a reportable segment.
- BNPL competitive pressure escalating: December 2025 multi-state AG inquiry sent to six BNPL providers (not Four); New York enacted BNPL licensing and ability-to-repay legislation in May 2025.

#### Risk-factor changes
- New risk added for Purchasing Power (acquired January 2, 2026): payroll-deduction model creates unique exposure to employer headcount reductions, payroll system changes, wage-assignment laws, and supply chain/carrier disruptions not previously relevant to the company.
- Heightened immigration policy risk explicitly added: potential changes under the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" and federal immigration enforcement cited as factors that could reduce the subprime consumer population and suppress demand.
- Purchasing Power's reliance on non-recourse securitizations and warehouse facilities added as a distinct debt/liquidity risk; structural or performance trigger breaches could impair receivable origination.
- AI-specific risk formalized: new language warns of accuracy issues, unintended bias, discriminatory outcomes, and evolving AI regulation as material concerns given increased deployment of generative AI tools across operations.

#### Material events
- Acquired Purchasing Power on January 2, 2026 for an undisclosed price; Purchasing Power is a voluntary employee-benefit/payroll-deduction retailer serving millions of employees and is expected to represent >30% of consolidated 2026 revenue (Progressive Leasing guided to drop below 70% share).
- On February 6–17, 2026, the company established non-recourse funding debt (secured) at a subsidiary level and simultaneously drew on/modified its revolving credit facility, per subsequent-event disclosures dated February 6, February 17, and February 18, 2026.
- Big Lots (bankruptcy September 2024, liquidation December 2024) and American Signature/Value City Furniture (bankruptcy November 2025, subsequent liquidation) both cited as former top-10 POS partners whose store closures negatively impacted 2025 results and are expected to further pressure 2026 performance.
- Share repurchase program remained active through December 31, 2025; aggregate repurchases from program inception through 2022–2025 disclosed but specific 2025 dollar amount not provided in the truncated text; 39,575,810 shares outstanding as of February 12, 2026.
