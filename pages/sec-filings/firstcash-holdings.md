#### Key financials

#### Segment & competitive notes
- U.K. Pawn segment (H&T, acquired August 14, 2025) contributed $101.7M revenue and $39.2M pre-tax income (39% margin) in Q1 2026, its first full comparable quarter; pawn loans were $215.4M and inventories $83.2M as of March 31, 2026.

<summary><strong>10-K</strong> — 2026-02-09</summary>

**FirstCash Holdings, Inc. (FCFS) — 10-K Summary (FY 2025, filed 2026-02-09)**

---

#### Key financials
- Pawn loan fees accounted for 46% of consolidated net revenue in FY 2025; gross profit from pawn merchandise sales accounted for 39%; AFF (retail POS payment solutions) contributed 15% of consolidated net revenues.
- Average pawn loan balance per transaction at December 31, 2025: $312 (U.S.), $112 (Latin America), $825 (U.K.); company operated 3,330 pawn stores as of December 31, 2025, up from 3,026 at the start of 2025.
- Aggregate market cap of non-affiliate shares was approximately $5.217 billion as of June 30, 2025; shares outstanding were 43,984,696 as of January 28, 2026.
- Debt capital structure includes 4.625% Senior Notes due 2028, 5.625% Senior Notes due 2030, and 6.875% Senior Notes due 2032 (issued February 21, 2024), plus revolving credit facility extended to 2029 (amended August 8, 2024) and secured term loans due 2027, 2029, and 2031.

---

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Four reportable segments: U.S. Pawn (1,207 stores in 29 states + D.C.), Latin America Pawn (1,837 stores across Mexico, Guatemala, El Salvador, Colombia), U.K. Pawn (286 stores in England, Scotland, Wales — new in 2025 via H&T acquisition), and Retail POS Payment Solutions (AFF, U.S. only).
- AFF operates ~16,400 active merchant partner locations; vertical mix shifted materially — automotive grew to 25% of gross transaction volumes in 2025 (vs. 19% in 2023) while furniture fell to 23% (vs. 48% in 2023); bank-originated installment loans were 45% of AFF gross transaction volumes in 2025; LTO was 43%; RISA was 12%.
- U.S. pawn industry estimated at 12,000–14,000 total stores; Mexico at 8,000–9,000; U.K. at slightly under 1,000 — all remain highly fragmented; only two publicly-held U.S.-based pawnshop operators exist (including FCFS).
- AFF launched a new off-balance-sheet (OBS) unsecured installment loan product in Q3 2025, fully retained by its Utah bank partner; AFF services these loans but bears credit risk only if loans become 90+ days past due.
