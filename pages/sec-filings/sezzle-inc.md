<summary><strong>8-K</strong> — 2026-05-12</summary>

#### Key financials
- As of May 7, 2026, Sezzle had **$153.5 million outstanding** under its prior revolving credit facility with Bastion Funding VI, LP; no amounts were repaid at closing and obligations were carried forward into the amended facility.
- New Revolving Credit Facility has a borrowing capacity of up to **$300 million**, with an option to increase by an additional **$75 million**, maturing **May 7, 2029**.
- Interest rate is **3-month Term SOFR + 3.86%**, with a 3-month Term SOFR floor of **2.00%**; minimum utilization is **$50 million**.

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Advance rate is **92.5%** of eligible pledged receivables, stepping down to **85%** if the Weighted Average Loss Rate on the three most recent Seasoned Vintages reaches **3.75% or more**, indicating credit-quality sensitivity in the receivables portfolio.
- Restricted payments (dividends, buybacks) are now permitted provided trailing twelve-month consolidated net income is positive and aggregate payments do not exceed **$75 million plus 50% of consolidated net income** after May 7, 2026.

#### Risk-factor changes
- New financial maintenance covenants on the Consolidated Group covering tangible net worth, liquidity, and leverage; failure triggers an event of default under the Revolving Credit Facility.
- An immediate event of default is triggered if defaulted or past-due collateral receivable ratios for a given vintage or collection period exceed pre-determined thresholds, adding vintage-level credit performance risk.

#### Material events
- On **May 7, 2026**, Sezzle Funding SPE II, LLC executed the Amended and Restated Revolving Credit and Security Agreement with Bastion Funding VI, LP as administrative agent, replacing the prior facility.
- On **May 7, 2026**, Sezzle executed **Amendment No. 3** to the Limited Guaranty and Indemnity Agreement, updating restricted payment covenants to permit shareholder returns subject to net income and aggregate cap conditions.

<summary><strong>10-Q</strong> — 2026-05-07</summary>

#### Key financials
- Q1 2026 total revenue of $135.5M (+29.2% vs. $104.9M in Q1 2025), driven by subscription revenue up 41.7% to $33.2M and income from other sources up 57.0% to $36.6M; operating income was $69.0M vs. $49.9M prior year.
- Net income of $51.3M ($1.47 diluted EPS) vs. $36.2M ($1.00 diluted EPS) in Q1 2025; effective tax rate 22.3% vs. 23.1%.
- Allowance for credit losses fell to $19.8M at March 31, 2026 from $28.5M at December 31, 2025, reflecting better-than-expected portfolio performance; provision for credit losses was $13.7M (10.1% of revenue) vs. $12.8M (12.2%) a year ago; gross charge-offs were $25.1M vs. $19.7M.
- Cash and equivalents rose to $120.4M at March 31, 2026 from $64.1M at December 31, 2025; operating cash flow was $89.0M; line of credit outstanding was $145.5M with $69.0M unused capacity on a $225M facility at an effective rate of 10.40% vs. 12.02% in Q1 2025.

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Single reportable segment (North America BNPL/payments); GMV grew 37.3% YoY to $1.11B in Q1 2026 vs. $808.7M in Q1 2025, driven by subscription product usage and consumer acquisition marketing (marketing spend doubled to $11.2M from $5.3M).
- Active Consumers reached 3.105M at March 31, 2026 (+1.9% from 3.049M at December 31, 2025); Active Subscribers stood at 0.7M; MODS declined 3.3% to 887K sequentially due to post-holiday seasonality.
- Loans purchased from originating partner totaled $981.2M order value ($749.4M carrying value) in Q1 2026 vs. $706.8M ($518.7M) in Q1 2025, indicating significant platform volume growth through the bank-partner channel.
- Sezzle filed a federal antitrust lawsuit against Shopify on June 9, 2025 alleging anticompetitive conduct in BNPL placement on its e-commerce platform; a motion-to-dismiss hearing was held December 8, 2025 with no ruling as of filing date—outcome could affect merchant distribution access.

#### Risk-factor changes
- No material changes to risk factors from those disclosed in the 2025 Form 10-K, per Item 1A.
- Ongoing material weakness in internal controls over financial reporting remains unremediated as of March 31, 2026: controls over cash-flow classification of notes receivable were deficient, causing restatement of cash flow statements for multiple prior periods (Q1–Q3 2024 and 2025, and FY 2024); remediation enhancements have been introduced but not yet validated as effective.

#### Material events
- Board authorized a $100M share repurchase program on December 12, 2025; during Q1 2026, Sezzle repurchased 393,776 shares at an average price of $65.40, spending $25.7M total, leaving ~$75.2M remaining capacity as of March 31, 2026.
- Sezzle launched "Sezzle Mobile" in 2026—an MVNO mobile phone plan (unlimited talk/text/5G on AT&T's network) embedded within the Sezzle app—representing a new product extension beyond core BNPL.
- New CFO Lee Brading joined under an employment agreement dated January 26, 2026 (incorporated by reference via 8-K filed January 29, 2026).
