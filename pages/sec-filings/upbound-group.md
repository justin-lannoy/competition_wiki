#### Key financials
- Q1 2026 total revenues of $1.219B, up 3.7% from $1.176B in Q1 2025; net earnings of $35.8M ($0.61 diluted EPS) vs. $24.8M ($0.42 diluted EPS) in Q1 2025.
- Operating profit improved to $77.4M from $62.6M in Q1 2025; gross profit rose to $586.5M from $550.1M; interest expense declined slightly to $26.9M from $27.8M.
- Cash from operations of $170.7M (vs. $148.0M in Q1 2025); cash ended at $98.4M vs. $120.5M at Dec. 31, 2025, after net debt repayment of ~$131M in Q1 2026.
- Total debt at March 31, 2026: Term Loan $870.6M (rate 6.42%), ABL $135.0M (rate 5.76%), Senior Notes $450M (6.375%, due Feb. 2029); legal accruals rose to $74.2M from $72.0M at Dec. 31, 2025.

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Acima (virtual LTO) Q1 2026 revenues $648.7M (+1.8% YoY), segment operating profit $77.3M vs. $73.7M; lease charge-offs $57.3M vs. $56.7M; primary growth driver for UPBD and direct competitor to Snap Finance in point-of-sale LTO.
- Brigit (financial health/EWA) contributed $67.7M in subscription/fee revenues in Q1 2026 vs. $31.9M for the partial period (Feb.–Mar.) in Q1 2025; segment operating profit $18.6M vs. $8.8M; customer cash advances grew to $63.7M from $59.0M at Dec. 31, 2025, with 74% current (0–15 days).
- Rent-A-Center Q1 2026 revenues $481.6M (down 1.5% YoY), segment operating profit $62.3M vs. $66.4M; e-commerce represented ~28% of LTO revenues vs. ~27% in Q1 2025, signaling continued channel shift.
- Mexico segment swung to a segment operating loss of $(0.1M) in Q1 2026 vs. profit of $1.2M in Q1 2025 on revenues of $21.8M vs. $18.2M, pressured by FX and cost headwinds.

#### Risk-factor changes
- Multistate/DC Attorneys General investigation (43 states) of Acima's LTO practices: DC settlement finalized in Q1 2026; company states it is "nearing a non-binding agreement in principle" with the Multistate Executive Committee, but no binding deal exists; $74.2M legal accrual as of March 31, 2026 includes expected Multistate monetary relief.
- New tariff/trade policy risk explicitly added: the filing flags "ongoing changes in tariff policies, including impacts from tariffs proposed or imposed by the current U.S. Presidential Administration on the price of imported goods" as a material forward-looking risk to costs and target consumers.
