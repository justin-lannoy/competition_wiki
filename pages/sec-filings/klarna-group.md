### 20-F — 2026-02-26

I can only work with the text provided in the filing. The XBRL data and narrative text provided are from Klarna Group plc (ticker: KLAR), not Snap Finance. Based solely on what was submitted, here is the summary:

---

#### Key financials
- Klarna reported a net loss of **$273 million in 2025** and **$244 million in 2023**, compared to a net profit of **$21 million in 2024**, reflecting renewed investment-driven losses after a brief return to profitability.
- Revenue is derived from four streams: transaction revenue, consumer service revenue (including "Fair Financing" fees and snooze fees), gain on sale of consumer receivables, and interest income; geographic mix includes U.S., Germany, and UK as named segments alongside "other countries."
- Klarna issued new senior unsecured bonds under its SMTN and EMTN programs in June 2025, including a SEK 600M tranche due 2027 and a SEK 900M tranche due 2028 (both STIBOR-linked), adding to earlier 2024 issuances of SEK 500M (due 2026), SEK 750M (due 2026), and SEK 250M (due 2027).

#### Segment & competitive notes
- Klarna operates as a single reportable segment; revenue is broken down geographically with the U.S., Germany, and UK disclosed separately, with U.S. operations explicitly noted as growing faster than more mature European markets but still dilutive to transaction margin percentage.
- Named competitors include Affirm, Block, PayPal, AfterPay, Revolut, NuBank, Shopify, Amazon, Walmart, and major card networks (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Capital One, Discover) as well as large card-issuing banks (JPMorgan Chase, Citibank, Bank of America, HSBC, BNP Paribas, Barclays, Santander).

#### Risk-factor changes
- New/elevated risk flagged around **AI and generative AI** usage, including risks of biased outputs, data rights deficiencies, regulatory non-compliance, and reputational harm from the customer service chatbot and shopping assistant.
- Elevated risk around **multi-class share capital structure** and equity-based compensation social security tax obligations, particularly in Sweden and the UK where employer social security on equity awards is uncapped, noted as potentially material depending on share price movements.

#### Material events
- Klarna completed its **IPO on the New York Stock Exchange on September 10, 2025** (ticker: KLAR), with an underwriter option exercised on **September 22, 2025**; total ordinary shares outstanding at filing date: **377,507,910**.
- Klarna acquired **KCO** (referenced as "KCOMember") effective **October 1, 2024**, with goodwill recognized on that date; subsidiary Klarna Group plc structure was reorganized ahead of the IPO, including conversion of Class B shares and deferred shares on **September 9, 2025**.
